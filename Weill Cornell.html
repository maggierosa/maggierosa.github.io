Our Story

Weill Cornell Medicine is committed to excellence in patient care, scientific discovery and the education of future physicians in New York City and around the world. Since 1898, our doctors and scientists have been engaged in world-class clinical care and cutting-edge research that connect patients to the latest treatment innovations and prevention strategies. Weill Cornell Medicine's powerful network of collaborators extends to its parent university Cornell University; to Qatar, where Weill Cornell Medicine-Qatar offers a Cornell University medical degree; and to programs in Tanzania, Haiti, Brazil, Austria and Turkey. Weill Cornell Medicine faculty provide exemplary patient care at NewYork-Presbyterian/Weill Cornell Medical Center, NewYork-Presbyterian Westchester Behavioral Health Center, NewYork-Presbyterian Lower Manhattan Hospital, NewYork-Presbyterian Queens and NewYork-Presbyterian Brooklyn Methodist Hospital. Weill Cornell Medicine is also affiliated with Houston Methodist.

Faculty Honors & Awards
  • Philip E. Stieg, Ph.D., M.D.
    Chairman of Neurological Surgery and the Margaret and Robert J. Hariri Professor of Neurological Surgery

    Dr. Philip Stieg, chairman of Neurological Surgery and the Margaret and Robert J. Hariri Professor of Neurological Surgery, was named by Albany Medical College as the Distinguished Alumnus of the college for 2022. This marked the first time the college honored one of its Ph.D. graduates with this award.  

    Research Profile Clinical Profile
  • Fabrizio Michelassi, M.D.
    Chairman of the Department of Surgery and the Lewis Atterbury Stimson Professor of Surgery

    Dr. Fabrizio Michelassi, chairman of the Department of Surgery and the Lewis Atterbury Stimson Professor of Surgery, was awarded the Founders Medal at the 63rd Annual Meeting of the Society for Surgery of the Alimentary Tract in May 2022. The society’s mission is to lead in advancing the science and practice of gastrointestinal surgery. 

    Research Profile Clinical Profile
  • Manikkam Suthanthiran, M.D.
    Chief of the Division of Nephrology and Hypertension and the Stanton Griffis Distinguished Professor of Medicine

    Dr. Manikkam Suthanthiran, chief of the Division of Nephrology and Hypertension and the Stanton Griffis Distinguished Professor of Medicine, received The Medawar Prize from The Transplantation Society for his outstanding contributions to the field of transplantation. It is the highest honor bestowed by the International Transplantation Society. Dr. Suthanthiran will be presented with the award at the TTS President's Plenary Session on Sept. 14 in Buenos Aires, Argentina.
